02

The Problem

📊
Reports ≠ Communication
ESG reports are written for regulators, not people. They're dense, technical, and impossible to turn into credible public communication without expert intervention.
48% of companies lack standard ESG metrics
⚠️
Marketing Tools Create Risk
Existing content tools optimise for persuasion, not compliance. Using them for ESG communication is a legal liability — not a communications strategy.
Regulators moving from guidance to enforcement in 2025
⚖️
ESG is Now a Risk Function
After EU Omnibus reform, scrutiny is higher than ever. One misleading ESG claim can trigger legal action, media backlash, and investor distrust simultaneously.
DWS fined €25M · Air France investigated · KLM penalised
🔴 Recent Greenwashing Enforcement Cases (2024–2025)
Deutsche Bank / DWS — €25M fine by German prosecutors for misleading investors about ESG credentials in marketing materials.
Air France, Lufthansa, KLM — scrutinised for "carbon neutral" flight claims based on questionable offsetting schemes under EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive.
Coca-Cola — forced by European Commission to revise "100% recycled plastic" packaging claims that excluded caps and labels.
HSBC exited Net-Zero Banking Alliance citing greenwashing exposure risk — signal that even avoidance of ESG claims is becoming a strategy.
